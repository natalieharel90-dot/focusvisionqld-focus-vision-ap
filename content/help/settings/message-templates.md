**What this is for:** Building a library of pre-written messages so staff can answer common patient questions quickly and consistently in the shared inbox.

## Where it lives

Message templates are managed under **Settings → Contact screen**, in the **Message Templates** section.

A **template** is a saved message — like medication instructions or appointment reminders — that staff insert into a reply instead of typing it each time.

## Adding a template

1. Open **Settings** and select the **Contact screen** tab.
2. Scroll to **Message Templates** and click **Add template**.
3. Enter a short **label**, choose a **category**, and write the message **body**.
4. Save.

## Editing, reordering, and disabling

1. Use the **up** and **down** arrows to reorder templates within their category.
2. Click **Disable** to hide a template from the inbox without deleting it; click **Enable** to bring it back.
3. Click the edit icon to change a template's label, category, or body.

:::tip
Keep template labels short and specific — staff scan labels, not the full text, when picking a reply.
:::

:::watch-out
Templates are general guidance, never diagnosis. The app gives no diagnoses. Keep wording educational and route clinical decisions through a surgeon.
:::

:::patient-sees
A patient sees template text as a normal message from clinic staff. There is no sign it came from a template.
:::
