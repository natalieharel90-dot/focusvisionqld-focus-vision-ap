**What this is for:** Showing staff how to report a privacy or security problem quickly, so the clinic can act before any harm spreads.

## What to report

Raise a concern if you notice anything like:

- A patient record that may have been seen by the wrong person.
- A message or document sent to the wrong patient.
- A lost or stolen device that was signed in.
- A login you suspect has been shared or used by someone else.
- Anything that feels like a mistake with patient data.

When in doubt, report it. A false alarm costs nothing; an unreported breach can cause real harm.

## How to report

1. Tell the **practice manager** straight away — in person or by phone, not by emailing patient details.
2. Write down what happened, when you noticed it, and which patient or record was involved.
3. Do **not** try to hide or undo it yourself — the audit log already has the facts.
4. If a device is lost or stolen, say so immediately so the clinic can sign it out.

## What happens next

The practice manager assesses the incident, contains it, and decides whether it must be reported to the regulator under the data-breach scheme. Acting fast gives the clinic the best chance to limit any impact.

:::tip
Reporting honestly and early is always the right move — including when the mistake was your own.
:::

:::watch-out
Don't discuss a suspected breach in patient messages or group chats. Keep details to the practice manager and the people handling it.
:::

:::patient-sees
Nothing — this is staff-internal. If a breach affects a patient, the clinic contacts them directly through the proper process.
:::
