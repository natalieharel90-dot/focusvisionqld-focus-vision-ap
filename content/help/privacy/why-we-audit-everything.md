**What this is for:** Explaining why the Recovery Companion records every staff action, and why that protects both patients and staff.

## What gets logged

Every action that touches a patient record creates an **audit entry**. That includes:

- Viewing a patient record.
- Editing patient or clinical details.
- Sending a message.
- Raising or resolving a manual flag.
- Changing routing rules or alert settings.

Each entry records who did it, what changed (before and after values), and the time, device, and network address.

## Append-only and kept for 7 years

The audit log is **append-only** — entries can never be edited or deleted, by anyone. Entries are kept for **7 years** after a patient is discharged, matching health-record retention requirements.

## Why it matters

- **Patient safety.** If a question arises about a patient's care, the log shows exactly what happened.
- **Staff protection.** An honest record proves you acted correctly and within your role.
- **Privacy compliance.** It lets the clinic show regulators that patient data is handled properly.

:::tip
Treat the audit log as a safety net, not surveillance. Doing your job normally creates a clean, helpful record.
:::

:::watch-out
Because viewing a record is logged, only open records for patients in your care. Browsing out of curiosity is visible and not permitted.
:::

:::patient-sees
Nothing — this is staff-internal. The audit log is never shown to patients.
:::
