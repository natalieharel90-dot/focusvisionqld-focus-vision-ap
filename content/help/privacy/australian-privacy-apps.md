**What this is for:** Giving every staff member a plain-English picture of the privacy rules that govern patient data in the Recovery Companion.

## What the APPs are

The **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)** are the national rules for handling personal information. Health information is treated as **sensitive information** — the strictest category. As an ophthalmology clinic, Focus Vision must follow them.

## What this means day to day

- **Only collect what you need.** Don't record extra patient details "just in case".
- **Use data only for care.** Patient information is for treating that patient — not for marketing or anything unrelated.
- **Keep it accurate.** Correct wrong details when a patient tells you.
- **Keep it secure.** See the encryption article for how the system protects data.

## Data stays in Australia

All patient data is stored in Australia — the system runs in the Sydney region. The app uses no overseas analytics tools. This **data residency** rule is non-negotiable.

## Patient access and correction

Patients have a right to see the information held about them and to ask for corrections. Pass any such request to the practice manager.

:::tip
A simple test before any action: "Is this in the patient's interest, for their care, and something they would expect?" If not, stop and ask.
:::

:::watch-out
Never copy patient data into personal email, spreadsheets, or messaging apps. That moves it outside the protected system and may breach the APPs.
:::

:::patient-sees
Nothing — this is staff-internal. Patients see the protections through the clinic's published privacy policy.
:::
