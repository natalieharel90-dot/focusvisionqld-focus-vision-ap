**What this is for:** Setting out the things staff must never do in the Recovery Companion — limits built in deliberately to protect patients.

## Don't share logins

Each staff member has their own account. **Never share a login or password.** The audit log records actions against whoever is signed in, so a shared login makes the record meaningless and leaves you accountable for someone else's actions.

## Don't view records you aren't caring for

Only open patient records for patients in your care. Every view is logged. Browsing out of curiosity — including for friends, family, or public figures — is not permitted.

## Don't give caregivers or family access

The app has **no family or caregiver access**, and this was removed on purpose for safeguarding reasons. Do not look for workarounds, do not share a patient's login with a relative, and do not add it back.

## Don't diagnose through the app

The app collects symptom data and routes patients to zones, but it gives **no diagnoses**. Staff must not use messages to diagnose or prescribe. Clinical decisions come from a surgeon, communicated as care guidance — not as an app feature.

## Don't move data out of the system

Don't screenshot, download, email, or paste patient data into other tools. That removes every protection the system provides.

:::tip
When unsure whether something is allowed, ask the practice manager before acting — not after.
:::

:::watch-out
Breaking these rules can be a reportable privacy breach. The audit log will show what happened, when, and on whose account.
:::

:::patient-sees
Nothing — this is staff-internal. These limits protect patients quietly in the background.
:::
