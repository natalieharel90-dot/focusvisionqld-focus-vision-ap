**What this is for:** Adding a patient to the triage queue yourself when something needs attention that a daily check-in did not catch.

## When to use a manual flag

The routing engine only reacts to check-in answers. Use a manual flag when a concern comes from somewhere else — a phone call, a message thread, a surgeon's note, or a patient who missed their check-in entirely.

## How to raise a manual flag

1. Open the patient's record from the patient list.
2. Find the **manual flag** section on the patient detail page.
3. Choose an **alert level** — yellow, orange, or red.
4. Type a clear **reason** — this is required and is shown on the triage card.
5. Submit the flag.

The patient now appears in the triage queue at the tier you chose, with your reason as the card detail.

## How a manual flag differs from a check-in flag

A manual flag sets a staff alert level only. It does **not** change the patient's zone screen — manual flags are decoupled from what the patient sees.

:::patient-sees
Nothing — a manual flag is staff-internal. The patient's app does not change when you flag them.
:::

:::watch-out
Write a specific reason. "Concern" tells the next staff member nothing — "Patient reports new floaters since 2pm call" tells them everything.
:::

:::tip
A red manual flag carries the same urgency as a red check-in. Only use it for genuinely urgent situations.
:::
