**What this is for:** Reading the triage queue so you can see, at a glance, which patients need staff attention right now.

## What the triage queue shows

The triage queue lists every patient currently flagged for staff attention. A patient lands here when a check-in routes to yellow, orange, or red, or when a staff member raises a manual flag.

Resolved items drop off automatically — the queue only ever shows open work.

## The three tiers

Cards are grouped and sorted by urgency:

- **Red — act immediately.** The most urgent. The patient sees only the orange screen.
- **Orange — contact today.** Reach the patient before the end of the day.
- **Yellow — review within 4h.** Check the details and respond within four hours.

The four stat cards at the top count Red, Orange, Yellow, and how many items were resolved today.

## Reading a card

Each card shows the patient's name, recovery day, and procedure, the time it was flagged, the surgeon, and a **Reported** line with the patient's pain, light sensitivity, vision, and symptom answers. Manual flags show the staff member's written reason instead.

Each card has three actions: **Message** opens the patient's thread, **Open** opens their full record, and **Resolve** clears the card.

:::tip
Use the filter at the top right to view just one tier — handy when a colleague is working the red cards and you take the yellows.
:::
