**What this is for:** Understanding what a daily check-in collects so you can read triage cards and patient records with confidence.

## What a check-in is

A daily check-in is a short, two-minute form the patient completes in their app once a day during recovery. It is how the clinic keeps an eye on every patient between appointments without anyone needing to phone them.

Each check-in is tied to the patient's **recovery day** — the number of days since their surgery date.

## What the patient submits

The patient answers four things:

1. **Vision** — whether it feels **better**, **same**, or **worse** than yesterday.
2. **Pain** — a number from **0** (none) to **5** (severe).
3. **Light sensitivity** — a number from **0** (none) to **5** (severe).
4. **Symptom chips** — they tap any unusual symptoms that apply (for example "discharge" or "flashes of light").

There is also an optional free-text box and, for some procedures, an optional eye photo.

## What happens next

When the patient submits, the routing engine reads every answer and decides two things: a **patient zone** (what the patient sees) and a **staff alert level** (whether you need to act). These two are decoupled — see the next article.

:::patient-sees
The patient sees a calm result screen confirming their check-in and showing guidance for their zone.
:::

:::tip
A check-in with no concerning answers still gets recorded — it builds the patient's recovery history for analytics and per-surgeon reports.
:::
