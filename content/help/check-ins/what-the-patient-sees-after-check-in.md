**What this is for:** Knowing exactly what the patient sees after a check-in, so you can answer their questions and trust the calm-by-design approach.

## The result screen

As soon as the patient submits, they see a result screen for their zone. The screen always opens with a colour hero, a reassuring headline, and their recovery day. Patients see one of three zones — green, yellow, or orange. **They never see red.**

## Green — on track

The patient sees a green hero ("You're tracking beautifully"), an optional message from the care team, and a list of **expected symptoms** for this stage of recovery. The tone is positive and reassuring.

## Yellow — take it easy

The patient sees an amber hero ("On track — let's take it easy"), a care-team message, a **"Take it easy today"** tip, and the expected-symptoms list. The message is gently cautious, not alarming.

## Orange — let's have a chat today

The patient sees an orange hero ("Let's have a chat today"), a care-team message, the clinic's phone number with a one-tap **Call the clinic now** button, and an after-hours number if one is set.

## Red is invisible to the patient

When a check-in routes to red, staff get an urgent alert — but the patient still sees the calm **orange** screen. This is deliberate, so an anxious patient is encouraged to call rather than panic.

:::patient-sees
A calm, colour-coded result screen with care-team guidance for their zone — green, yellow, or orange only.
:::

:::tip
The headline and message come from Recovery guidance, scoped by procedure and surgeon. Edit those to change what patients read here.
:::
