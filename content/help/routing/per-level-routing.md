**What this is for:** Setting a separate outcome for each step on the pain and light-sensitivity scales.

## Graded answers

Some check-in questions are graded — the patient picks a number on a 0-5 scale. Pain and light sensitivity both work this way.

Each of the six levels (0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5) is its **own rule row** with its own route. This lets you draw the line wherever your clinic wants it.

For example, on pain you might set:

- Levels 0-2 → **Off** (expected discomfort)
- Level 3 → **Yellow** (higher than expected)
- Level 4 → **Orange** (concerning)
- Level 5 → **Red** (patient distressed)

## How to edit a graded item

1. Open **Settings → Alert thresholds**.
2. Find the **Pain** or **Light sensitivity** group.
3. For each level row, pick **Off**, **Yellow**, **Orange**, or **Red**.
4. Click **Save** for that group.

Each row shows a short scale label (such as "Moderate — higher than expected") to help you judge severity.

:::patient-sees
The patient picks one number. Whichever route that level carries decides their screen — they never see the scale of rules behind it.
:::

:::tip
Keep the routes climbing in step with the numbers. A higher level should never be a milder route than a lower one.
:::

:::watch-out
Vision answers (Better / Same / Worse) are not graded — each option has a single rule, not a scale.
:::
