**What this is for:** Knowing what patients can adjust in Settings, so you can guide them when they want the app to feel more comfortable.

## What patients can change

The Settings screen is grouped into sections. Changes save automatically and apply instantly — no reload.

- **Appearance** — a dark mode toggle, plus a choice of **five colour themes** (Calm medical, Premium clinical, Bright & friendly, Sand & terracotta, Minimal modern).
- **Reminders** — toggles for medication reminders, the daily check-in reminder, and message notifications; a snooze duration; an optional friendly nudge; quiet hours; a travel timezone lock; and the **lock screen widget**.
- **Language** — switches the app language.
- **Accessibility** — larger text, higher contrast, reduce motion, and voice control for hands-free check-ins and dose marking. Also a link to replay the app tour.
- **Account** — name and contact details.
- **Privacy & data** — privacy policy, download my data, and account deletion.

## The lock screen widget

The lock screen widget toggle shows the patient's next dose on their phone's lock screen, so they can see it without unlocking.

:::patient-sees
The most useful thing to know: appearance and accessibility settings are personal comfort choices. A patient post-op with light sensitivity often turns on dark mode and larger text — encourage it, it makes the app easier on sore eyes.
:::

:::watch-out
Reminder toggles here control whether notifications are sent at all. If a patient says they get no reminders, check these toggles before assuming a phone problem.
:::
