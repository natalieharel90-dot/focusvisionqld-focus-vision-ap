**What this is for:** Understanding the daily check-in from the patient's side, so you can reassure patients about what it asks and what happens after.

## The check-in questions

The check-in is one short form, around two minutes long. The patient answers:

- **Vision today** — Worse, Same or Better, compared to yesterday
- **Eye pain level** — a 0 to 5 scale, None to Severe
- **Light sensitivity** — a 0 to 5 scale, None to Severe
- **Any unusual symptoms?** — No keeps the card closed; Yes reveals symptom chips to tap, plus an "Other" option with a free-text box
- **Eye photo** — an optional photo upload, only if your clinic has enabled it

If symptoms are severe, the symptoms card shows a prompt to call the clinic straight away rather than waiting.

## The result screen

After submitting, the patient lands on a result screen with one of three calming outcomes:

- **Green** — "You're tracking beautifully"
- **Yellow** — "On track — let's take it easy", with a take-it-easy tip
- **Orange** — "Let's have a chat today", with the clinic phone number and a call button

Green and Yellow results also show expected symptoms for this stage and any message from the care team.

:::patient-sees
Patients never see a red screen. Even when a check-in triggers an urgent staff alert, the patient still sees the calm Orange result. The alarming colour is staff-side only — patients in Orange are already anxious, and a red screen would discourage them from calling.
:::

:::tip
A completed check-in is saved and viewable later under My documents, in the check-in history. You can point a patient there to review what they reported.
:::
