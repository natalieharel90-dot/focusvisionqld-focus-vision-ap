**What this is for:** Giving staff a quick mental map of the patient app, so you can picture what a patient is looking at when they call or message you.

## What the patient app is

The patient app is a calm mobile web app — a Progressive Web App (PWA) the patient installs by adding it to their phone's home screen. There is nothing to download from an app store. It is deliberately quiet: soft colours, short screens, and no clutter.

## How patients move around

Every screen sits behind a fixed **bottom navigation bar** with five tabs:

- **Home** — greeting, recovery day, check-in prompt, feature tiles
- **Check-in** — the daily symptom check
- **Medications** — eye-drop schedule and reminders
- **Messages** — one thread with your clinic
- **Settings** — themes, reminders, accessibility, account

The currently selected tab is highlighted in the patient's chosen theme colour.

## What patients see beyond the tabs

From the Home tiles patients also reach **Videos & info**, **My documents**, **Contact clinic**, and (if enabled) a feedback screen and pre-op information.

:::patient-sees
The whole app follows the patient's chosen theme and dark-mode setting — so two patients describing the same screen may see different colours. Focus on the screen name and content, not the colour.
:::

:::tip
If a patient says they "can't find" something, ask which of the five bottom tabs they are on. Naming the tab usually resolves it instantly.
:::
