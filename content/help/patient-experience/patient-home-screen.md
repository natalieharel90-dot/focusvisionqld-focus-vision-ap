**What this is for:** Knowing exactly what a patient sees first when they open the app, so you can talk them through it over the phone.

## What's at the top

The home screen opens with a time-aware greeting ("Good morning, Sarah") and a small pill badge showing where the patient is in their journey:

- After surgery — **Day X of recovery** with their procedure type
- Before surgery — a countdown to their surgery date

## The cards and tiles

Below the greeting, in order:

- **Next appointment card** — the upcoming appointment with date, type, clinician and location, plus an "Add to my calendar" link. If no time is booked yet, it says the clinic will confirm one.
- **Surgeon Spotlight** — a card linking to a personal welcome video from the patient's surgeon. Only appears after surgery, and only if the surgeon has recorded one.
- **Today's check-in** — a large prompt to start the daily check-in, or a "done for today" confirmation once completed.
- **Feature tiles** — a 2-column grid: Medications, Videos & info, My documents, Contact clinic, Settings, and (if enabled) Leave feedback.
- **Pre-op information** — shown only before surgery day.

Tiles can carry a small badge — for example, the number of doses due today, or unread messages on Contact clinic.

:::patient-sees
The home screen adapts to where the patient is. A pre-op patient and a Day-5 patient see different cards. If a patient describes a card you don't expect, check whether they have had surgery yet.
:::

:::watch-out
The Day-X number counts from the surgery date in the patient's record. If it looks wrong, the surgery date on their procedure is likely the cause — not a bug in the app.
:::
