**What this is for:** Knowing what lives in a patient's documents area and how the watermark works, so you can answer questions about their paperwork.

## What's in My documents

The documents screen gathers everything in one place, in sections:

- **Clinic documents** — surgery reports, prescriptions, receipts and aftercare papers, grouped by category
- **Check-in history** — every daily check-in, showing the day, zone result, pain score and vision; tapping one reopens that result screen
- **Recovery photos** — any eye photos the patient uploaded, with the date and which check-in they belong to

If a patient has nothing yet, the screen explains that clinic documents and their own check-ins and photos will appear here.

## How viewing works

Tapping a clinic document opens it inside the app — PDFs and images preview directly. Each file is fetched through a short-lived secure link, so documents are never publicly accessible.

## The watermark

Every document the patient opens is **watermarked at view time** with the patient's name and the current date. The watermark is an overlay added when the document is displayed — the stored file itself is never changed. A note under the viewer tells the patient their name and today's date are on it.

:::patient-sees
The watermark only appears when the patient is viewing the document. It is a privacy and accountability measure, not a sign anything is wrong — patients sometimes ask why their name is "stamped" on a file.
:::

:::tip
If a patient says a document is missing, check whether your clinic has uploaded it to their record. The app only shows what has been shared to that patient.
:::
