**What this is for:** Understanding how messaging looks to the patient, so the conversation feels consistent whichever side you're on.

## What patients see

The patient has a single message thread with the clinic. There are no folders, no separate conversations, and no choosing who to write to. The screen shows:

- A header reading **Focus Vision team** with "Usually replies within 2 hours"
- The full conversation history, oldest at the top
- A composer at the bottom for typing a message and adding an attachment

Patients can attach a photo or file. Replies appear in real time — if a staff member sends a message while the patient has the screen open, it shows up immediately.

## What's the same as the staff side

It is the **same thread**. Every message you send from the shared inbox lands here, and every message the patient sends lands in your inbox. Attachments work both ways.

## What's different

- Patients see **one** thread; staff see an inbox of many.
- Patients see staff messages labelled with a name and role; staff see internal context the patient never does.
- Patients cannot see staff notes, resolved/unresolved status, or quick-reply templates — those are staff-only.
- Opening the thread automatically marks staff messages as read.

:::patient-sees
To the patient this is just a friendly chat with "the Focus Vision team" — they don't see which staff member picks it up, or that it's a triaged inbox. Keep replies warm and plain.
:::

:::tip
The "replies within 2 hours" line sets the patient's expectation. If your clinic is running behind, a short holding message keeps patients reassured.
:::
