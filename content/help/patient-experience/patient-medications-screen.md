**What this is for:** Knowing how patients track their eye drops, so you can help when a patient asks about a dose or a reminder.

## What's on the screen

The medications screen shows the patient's eye-drop plan for the day:

- **Today's progress** — a card with doses taken out of total, a progress bar, and the next dose time
- **Today's schedule** — a timeline of every dose, each with a time, the medication, and a circle to tap when taken
- **Your reminder schedule** — each medication with its frequency, reminder times, and any tapering notes

The next dose due is highlighted. Taken doses show a tick. The due dose also offers a **Snooze** button.

## How reminders work

When a dose is due, the app sends a notification to the patient's phone. The patient must allow notifications once — the screen shows a "Turn on dose reminders" prompt for this.

- **Snooze** fires the reminder again after a set time (5, 10, 15 or 30 minutes — chosen in Settings).
- Reminder **times are set by the care team**, not the patient. To change them, the patient messages the clinic.
- Times follow the phone's clock, so they adjust automatically when the patient travels (unless they lock the timezone in Settings).

:::patient-sees
Medication reminders and marking doses taken work offline. If the patient has no signal, reminders still fire and taps are saved — everything syncs back when they reconnect.
:::

:::watch-out
If a patient blocked notifications, the app cannot re-ask. They must re-enable notifications in their phone's browser settings. The screen shows a "Reminders are blocked" warning when this is the case.
:::
