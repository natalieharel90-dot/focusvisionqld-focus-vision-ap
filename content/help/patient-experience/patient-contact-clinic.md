**What this is for:** Knowing what the Contact clinic screen offers patients, so you understand the routes a patient can use to reach you.

## What's on the Contact screen

The Contact clinic screen opens with a clinic header — your clinic name and a short tagline about service areas and hours. Below it is a list of tappable contact options, each one configured by your clinic in Settings. Depending on setup, a patient may see:

- **Call** — dials the clinic phone number
- **Message** — opens the in-app message thread
- **Book** — opens a booking link for a follow-up
- **Directions / map** — opens the clinic location in maps
- **Custom info rows** — informational entries with no action, such as opening hours

At the bottom is an **after-hours emergency notice** with an emergency phone number and a short message.

## What patients can do here

The Contact screen is the patient's directory for reaching you outside of messaging — for a phone call, directions, or a booking. The in-app message thread is also reachable from here for non-urgent questions.

:::patient-sees
The options a patient sees are exactly the ones your clinic has switched on in Settings. If a patient expects a "Book" button and doesn't see one, it has not been configured — it is not a fault on their phone.
:::

:::tip
Only call taps are recorded in the audit log. If you need to confirm a patient tried to call, the audit log will show it; opening maps or a booking link is not logged.
:::
