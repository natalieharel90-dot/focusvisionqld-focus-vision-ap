**What this is for:** Working out why a patient says a message did not arrive, and confirming it was actually sent.

Messages appear in the patient's **in-app messaging thread**. They do not go out by SMS or email. So the patient must open the app to read them — and they need notifications on to be alerted that a new one arrived.

## Things to check, in order

1. Open the patient's message thread and confirm the message shows as sent, not stuck in a draft.
2. Check the time stamp — make sure you sent it to the right patient's thread.
3. Confirm the patient has installed the app and can sign in.
4. Ask the patient to open the app and look in the **Messages** tab directly.
5. Ask them to check notifications are turned on for the app, so they are alerted next time.
6. Check Do Not Disturb or Focus mode is not silencing the app's notifications.
7. If it still does not appear, check the audit log to confirm the send was recorded.

:::patient-sees
A new message appears in the patient's Messages tab. If notifications are on, they also get an alert on their lock screen. If notifications are off, the message is still waiting in the app — but they will not know until they open it themselves.
:::

:::watch-out
Because messages are in-app only, a patient who never opens the app will not see anything. For urgent matters, do not rely on a message alone — use the clinic's phone contact process.
:::

:::tip
If a reply matters today, ask the patient by phone to open the app and check Messages.
:::
