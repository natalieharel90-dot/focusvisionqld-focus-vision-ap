**What this is for:** Knowing what the patient's side of messaging looks like, so you can guide them and set the right expectations.

## One simple conversation

In their app, the patient has a **Messages** tab with a single conversation labelled **Focus Vision**. They do not see a list of threads, staff names, or roles — just one calm, ongoing chat with the clinic.

Even though several staff may reply from the shared inbox, the patient experiences it as one consistent voice.

## Sending and receiving

The patient can:

1. Type a message and send it to the clinic at any time.
2. See staff replies appear in the same conversation.
3. Get a push notification when the clinic replies.

When a patient sends their first message, a new thread is created for staff in the shared inbox.

## What the patient does not see

The patient cannot see:

- Which staff member replied, or their role.
- Whether a thread is marked resolved.
- Quick-reply templates, internal notes, or triage cards.

:::patient-sees
A single, friendly conversation with Focus Vision, with push notifications for new replies.
:::

:::watch-out
Messaging is not for emergencies. If a patient describes an urgent problem, direct them to call the clinic — the orange check-in screen and the contact options exist for exactly this.
:::
