**What this is for:** Understanding every panel on a patient's record so you know where each piece of information lives.

## Header and progress

At the top, the **header** shows the patient's name, status, age, and contact details. Below it, a green **recovery progress** bar shows their day in a 90-day recovery window and their next follow-up.

## The panels

The record is laid out in panels across two columns.

1. **Patient details** — name, date of birth, Medicare, private health, emergency contact, and allergies. Has an **Edit** option.
2. **Procedures** — every surgical event, each with its own surgeon, eye, date, and recovery timeline. You can add another procedure here.
3. **Medications** — current eye drops and tablets, with dose and schedule. Stopped medications are kept in a history list.
4. **Appointments** — upcoming follow-ups. Unscheduled ones show as "to be booked".
5. **Internal staff notes** — observations and handovers between staff. Never visible to the patient.
6. **Daily check-in log** — the history of check-ins the patient submitted, with pain, vision, and light scores.
7. **Manual flags** — yellow, orange, or red flags staff have raised for review.
8. **Documents** — files on the patient's record, such as discharge letters and consent forms.

A **Patient app features** panel and a **Custom content** panel let you tailor what the patient sees in their app.

:::patient-sees
The patient sees their own medications, appointments, documents, check-in history, and pinned content in their app — but never your staff notes or manual flags.
:::

:::tip
Use **Quick actions** on the right column to call, message, or schedule for the patient without leaving the page.
:::
