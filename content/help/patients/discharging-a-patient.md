**What this is for:** Marking a patient's recovery as finished, so they no longer appear in active-recovery lists.

## When to discharge

Discharge a patient once their recovery is complete and they no longer need day-to-day monitoring. It is a tidy-up action — it does not delete anything.

## Steps

1. Open the patient's record.
2. In the **Quick actions** panel on the right, select **Mark as discharged**.
3. A confirmation appears. Select **Confirm — discharge [patient name]**.

The patient's status changes to **Discharged** and they drop out of active-recovery lists and counts.

## What happens to their record

- Their full clinical record is **kept** — procedures, medications, check-ins, notes, flags, and documents all remain.
- The audit log keeps every entry. Records are retained for 7 years after discharge.
- You can **re-admit** the patient at any time using the **Re-admit patient** action in Quick actions.

:::patient-sees
Daily check-in reminders stop, and the recovery journey closes off in their app. They keep access to their documents and recovery history.
:::

:::watch-out
Do not discharge a patient who still has open flags or an active concern. Resolve those first.
:::

:::tip
If you discharge someone by mistake, just open their record and select **Re-admit patient** — nothing is lost.
:::
