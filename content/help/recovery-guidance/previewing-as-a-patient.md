**What this is for:** Checking how a zone screen will look to a patient before you save your edits.

## Why preview

The editor shows your guidance as form fields. The patient sees something quite different — a phone-shaped screen with a coloured hero panel, symptom lists, and tip cards. Previewing lets you catch wording or layout problems before they reach a patient.

## How to preview

1. Open **Settings → Recovery guidance**.
2. Edit the fields in the zone panel you are working on.
3. Click **Preview as patient** at the bottom of that panel.
4. A phone-shaped mock opens, showing the screen exactly as the patient would read it.
5. Click **Close preview** to return to editing.

The preview reflects whatever is currently in the form, including unsaved changes — so you can fine-tune and re-preview until it reads well.

## What the preview shows

- The coloured zone hero with your headline and message.
- A "What's normal right now" list, one item per expected-symptom line.
- "Today's tip" and "What to do" cards, if those fields are filled.
- The warning note for Yellow and Orange.

Empty fields are simply left out, so the preview matches what the patient really gets.

:::patient-sees
Nothing — the preview is staff-only. The patient sees the real screen only after you save.
:::

:::tip
Preview each zone after every edit. Reading it in phone shape often reveals a headline that is too long or a tip that is unclear.
:::
