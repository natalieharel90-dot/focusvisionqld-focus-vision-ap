**What this is for:** Changing the words on the screens patients read after a check-in.

## Opening the editor

1. Open **Settings** from the sidebar.
2. Click the **Recovery guidance** tab.
3. You will see three editable panels — Green, Yellow, and Orange.

## The fields on each screen

Each zone screen is built from these fields:

- **Headline** — the large reassuring line at the top.
- **Message** — a short paragraph under the headline.
- **Expected symptoms** — one symptom per line; shown as a "What's normal right now" list.
- **Today's tip** — a single helpful suggestion.
- **Instructions** — a "What to do" paragraph.
- **Warning** — a cautionary note (Yellow and Orange only; Green has no warning).

## Saving your changes

1. Edit the fields in the zone panel you want to change.
2. Click **Preview as patient** to check how it reads.
3. Click **Save** for that zone.

Each zone saves on its own, so you can update Yellow without touching Green or Orange.

:::patient-sees
The next check-in that lands in that zone shows the updated wording right away.
:::

:::tip
Keep headlines short and calm. The patient reads this in a moment, not a long sit-down.
:::

:::watch-out
Guidance ships with placeholder text that needs clinical sign-off. Have a clinician review the wording before patients rely on it.
:::
