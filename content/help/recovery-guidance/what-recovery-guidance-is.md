**What this is for:** Understanding the screens a patient reads right after they finish a daily check-in.

## What recovery guidance is

Recovery guidance — also called "zone content" — is the page the patient sees once they submit a check-in. It reassures them, explains what is normal, and tells them what to do next.

There are three versions, one per patient zone:

- **Green — on track.** The check-in looked fine.
- **Yellow — keep an eye on this.** Something is worth watching; staff will review within 4 hours.
- **Orange — we'll be in touch.** Higher concern; staff will contact the patient today.

There is no Red screen. A Red routing outcome shows the patient the calm Orange screen while staff get the urgent alert separately.

## Where the patient sees it

The patient completes their check-in, the routing engine works out their zone, and the matching guidance screen appears immediately. It is the last thing they read before returning to their home screen.

Each screen can carry a headline, a message, a list of expected symptoms, a tip for the day, instructions, and (for Yellow and Orange) a warning note.

:::patient-sees
After every check-in the patient lands on one of three guidance screens — Green, Yellow, or Orange — chosen by their routing outcome.
:::

:::tip
Write guidance in warm, plain language. This is the patient's main reassurance after a possibly anxious check-in.
:::
