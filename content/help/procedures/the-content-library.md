**What this is for:** Building the clinic's set of educational videos, articles, and FAQs that patients see during recovery.

## What the content library holds

The **content library** is the clinic's shelf of educational items. Each item is one of three types: a **video**, an **article**, or an **FAQ** (a short question-and-answer). Items are reused across many patients rather than written fresh each time.

Every item is tagged two ways:

- **Procedure** — which procedure the item is about, such as LASIK or cataract.
- **Audience** — when the patient should see it: **pre-op** (before surgery), **post-op** (after surgery), or **both**.

These tags decide which patients an item can reach. Bulk push, for example, only offers post-op and "both" items because pushes go to recovering patients.

## How to add an item

1. Open the content library from the **Procedures** area.
2. Select **Add content** and choose the type — video, article, or FAQ.
3. Enter the title and the body text, or paste the media link for a video.
4. Tag the item with its procedure and its audience.
5. Save the item so it becomes available to attach to patients.

:::tip
Write titles as plain, reassuring questions or statements — patients scan them quickly. "Why does my eye feel gritty?" reads better than "Post-operative discomfort".
:::

:::watch-out
The content library is patient education only. It must never give a diagnosis or tell a patient to change treatment — clinical advice comes from staff messages.
:::

:::patient-sees
Items tagged for a patient's procedure and recovery stage appear in their app's learning area, ready to read or watch.
:::
