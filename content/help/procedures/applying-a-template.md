**What this is for:** Turning a procedure template's defaults into a real patient's medication list and appointment schedule in one step.

## What happens when you apply a template

Applying a template **materialises** its contents onto the patient. The system reads the template for the chosen surgeon and procedure, then creates a live medication for every default drop or tablet and a live appointment for every default follow-up. "Materialise" simply means the preset items become real, editable records on that patient.

The patient's surgery date anchors the schedule. An appointment set to Day +7 in the template lands seven days after surgery.

## How to apply one

1. Open the new patient's record, or add the patient first if they are not in the system.
2. Confirm the surgeon and procedure are correct — these decide which template applies.
3. Set the surgery date so appointment dates calculate correctly.
4. Select **Apply procedure template**.
5. Review the medications and appointments that appear.
6. Adjust anything that differs for this patient, then save.

:::tip
You can still edit, add, or stop individual medications after applying. The template is a starting point, not a lock.
:::

:::watch-out
If no template exists for that surgeon and procedure combination, nothing materialises. Create the template first in the Procedures library.
:::

:::patient-sees
The patient's app immediately shows their medication schedule with reminder times and their upcoming appointments on the home screen.
:::
