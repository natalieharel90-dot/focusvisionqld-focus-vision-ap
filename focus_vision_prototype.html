From your care team

Your symptoms today are completely within the range we'd expect for Day 4 after LASIK. Mild grittiness, slight light sensitivity, and fluctuating vision are all normal. Keep going — you're doing all the right things.

Expected symptoms around this time

  • Mild grittiness or "sand in the eye" feeling
  • Slight light sensitivity in bright environments
  • Vision that fluctuates through the day
  • Watery eyes, especially in wind or cold
  • Slight halos around lights at night
  • Mild dryness — keep using lubricating drops

Today's tip

Keep using artificial tears every 1–2 hours, even if your eyes feel fine. Hydration is the single biggest comfort factor in week one. Avoid rubbing your eyes — if they itch, use a cool compress instead.

How this prototype works

This is a clickable mockup — every tile, screen tab, and theme swatch is interactive. Show this to staff, surgeons or developers to demonstrate the experience.

  • Switch themes with the pills at the top, or from the patient's in-app Settings screen.
  • Toggle dark mode globally or per the in-app toggle.
  • Click any tile, gallery thumbnail, or bottom-nav icon to navigate the app.
  • Use the "Staff dashboard" tab to view the admin side of the system.

Production notes

  • Real app should use native iOS/Android (or React Native) for proper push notifications.
  • MFA via SMS through Twilio/MessageBird or AWS SNS — costs ~$0.05–0.10 per code.
  • HIPAA / Australian Privacy Act 1988 compliance required for patient data.
  • Spec doc accompanying this prototype has the full requirements.

About the staff mobile app

This is the Focus Vision Staff app, distributed via the Apple App Store and Google Play. Every clinical staff member can install it on their own phone for quick access while on shift, on-call, or away from the desk.

  • Same login as the web dashboard, with MFA. Face ID / fingerprint quick-unlock for return visits.
  • Shared inbox — every staff member sees every patient thread.
  • Push notifications when a patient sends a message or a check-in is flagged. Each staff member sets their own preferences in the Me tab.
  • Tap the bottom-nav tabs to switch between Messages, Triage, Patients and Me.
  • Read-only access to patient detail. Heavier edits (adding meds, changing procedures) stay on the web dashboard.
  • Quiet hours respect the on-call rota — emergencies always reach the on-call nurse.

  • Tap Triage at the bottom to see flagged patients.
  • Tap Patients for the patient list.

How the shared inbox works

  • 📬 Every Focus Vision staff member sees every patient thread — clinical staff and reception. Replies from other team members are visible to everyone, so anyone can pick up where a colleague left off. Reception can handle administrative messages (appointment changes, billing questions) which keeps clinical staff focused on clinical questions.
  • 👤 Each reply is labelled with the staff name and role so the patient knows who is helping them.
  • 🔔 Push notifications ping when a new patient message arrives (on the staff mobile app). Each staff member controls their own notification preferences in their personal settings — see below.
  • 🗂️ A thread can be assigned to a specific staff member if it needs focused follow-up, or marked Resolved when the patient's question is fully answered.

About this log

Every action staff take on a patient record is logged automatically — viewing, editing, messaging, manual flagging. Logs are immutable (records cannot be edited or deleted, only added) and retained for 7 years post-discharge in line with Australian clinical record retention standards. Only admin-role users can access this view. Anomaly detection flags unusual patterns (e.g. a single staff member accessing 50+ records in an hour) for the Privacy Officer to review.

How new patient setup works

  • 📱 Patient signs up via the app with email + phone. After MFA they appear here in the New patients queue.
  • 🔍 Staff confirm identity against your existing patient records (matches by email or phone, or manually linked).
  • 👤 Pick surgeon and procedure — the template auto-populates medications, appointments, and recovery content.
  • ✏️ Edit per-patient as needed — different astigmatism, atypical case, custom notes, etc.
  • ✅ Activate — patient moves out of this queue and into the active Patients list. They get a "Welcome — your care team has activated your account" notification.

Graded items — set the zone for each level independently

Every answer the patient can give — every value of a graded item (pain 0–5, light sensitivity 0–5), every vision option, and every symptom chip — has its own routing with four options:

Off — no flag
Yellow — patient sees Yellow, staff reviews within 4h
Orange — patient sees Orange, staff contacts today
Red — patient sees Orange (calming), staff get urgent alert immediately

If multiple answers fire, the most severe route wins (Red > Orange > Yellow > Off). Red is decoupled from what the patient sees — they always see Orange's calming "Let's have a chat today" screen even when staff get a Red urgent alert.

How procedure × surgeon overrides work — pick a procedure and/or a surgeon from the dropdowns at the top of this section to create an override. Only the rules you actually change get saved as part of the override; everything else falls back to the next level up (Default ruleset → procedure override → surgeon-specific override → procedure × surgeon override). Overridden rules show with a highlighted left-border so you can see at a glance what differs. Use this for procedures with genuinely different recovery patterns (e.g. PRK takes longer than LASIK, so "Vision Worse after Day" might be Day 7 for PRK, Day 3 for LASIK) or for surgeons whose clinical preferences differ from the clinic default.
